Positive Applications of Computing

Impacts on Communication

October 30th, 2023 by Christopher Massey

There are numerous ways in which computers have improved our ability to communicate as a species. The existence and widespread propagation of the internet has been pivotal to the way humans interact on a day-to-day basis.

From emails, instant messaging, social media, voice and video calls, to collaboration and teamwork, below are just some of the ways in which computers have improved our ability to communicate.

Widespread Adoption

Statistics show that:

4.66 Billion people rely on computers to communicate

That's no trivial amount. There's little doubt that computers have revolutionised communication by removing barriers such as not having enough time or money to meet in person, and providing the necessary convenience and flexibility to make communication easier.

Interpersonal communication

This means how computers have allowed invididual people more freedom to communicate. For example:

  • Computers have enabled us to instantly send and receive messages, be it through email, instant messaging, or social media. This could potentially be with people who live all around the world.
  • Computers have also enabled us to communicate with voice and video applications, such as skype, or zoom. This allows for much more expression than a text message can convey.

Collaboration

Whilst this is not limited to businesses, computers have also greatly improved our ability to collaborate and work on projects together.

  • The adoption of messaging and video conferenceing software such as Microsoft Teams have made work from home possible, reducing traffic, emissions, and the number of hours the average person spends commuting.
  • It also allows for collaboration between teams in different parts of the country, or even in different countries. Computers have enabled teams in completely different timezones to both work on the same project, vastly increasing productivity.

Speed

Unlike conventional methods, computers have vastly improved the speed at which we can communicate with each other.

  • Unlike post, computers enable you to be sent information almost instantaneously.
  • Messages can be received without the user having to be physically present.
